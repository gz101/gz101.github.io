My Harvard Journey

I have always had a passion for computers and have had an immense joy in messing around with them since I was a young boy. In early 2020 with the encouragement of friends from the tech industry, I was introduced to an option of undertaking a Master degree via distance learning at Harvard University's Extension School.

Keen to obtain the most complete and high-quality education possible, I have decided to tailor the incredibly flexible Master's program at Harvard to replicate the core curriculum of the undergraduate computer science students at Harvard. I have purposely chosen to take the insanely difficult path, through some of Harvard's most challenging courses in order to experience the learning journey of the world's brightest minds. I have listed my intended course plan below (although this is subject to Harvard's course offerings on a year-by-year basis):

  • CSCI E-121: Introduction to Theoretical Computer Science (CS121: Introduction to Theoretical Computer Science)
  • CSCI E-124: Data Structures and Algorithms (CS124: Data Structures and Algorithms)
